Europe is reassessing its energy strategy after navigating the severe energy crisis sparked by the reduction of Russian gas supplies. The continent managed to avoid the worst-case scenarios by rapidly diversifying its energy sources, including a major push for liquefied natural gas (LNG) and renewables. The article reviews the policies that worked and looks forward at the long-term challenges. These include ensuring energy security, managing high costs, and accelerating the green transition to prevent future crises.
