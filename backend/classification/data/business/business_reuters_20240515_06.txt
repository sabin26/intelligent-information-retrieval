U.S. retail sales were unexpectedly stagnant in April, raising new questions about the strength of consumer spending and the overall economy. The flat figures suggest that high interest rates and persistent inflation may be starting to weigh more heavily on household budgets. This slowdown in consumer activity could be an early indicator of a cooling economy. Economists and Federal Reserve officials are closely watching these trends for signs of whether the economy is heading for a soft landing or a more significant downturn.
