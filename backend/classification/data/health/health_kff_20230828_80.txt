Medical debt is a pervasive problem in the United States, placing a significant financial burden on millions of families. An investigation by KFF Health News reveals the far-reaching impact of this debt, which can ruin credit scores, deplete savings, and force people to delay or forgo necessary medical care. The article highlights how even those with health insurance can be saddled with crippling debt due to high deductibles and out-of-pocket costs. This issue drives inequality and creates a major barrier to both financial security and health.
