Public health officials are grappling with a health crisis related to vaping, especially among teenagers and young adults. While often marketed as a safer alternative to smoking, e-cigarettes are not harmless and can contain nicotine and other toxic chemicals. The article discusses the known health risks, including lung injury and nicotine addiction, and the regulatory challenges in controlling a rapidly evolving market of flavored products that appeal to youth. The long-term health effects of vaping are still largely unknown.
