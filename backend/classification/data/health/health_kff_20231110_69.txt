The future of telehealth in a post-pandemic world is uncertain, as temporary regulatory flexibilities that allowed for its expansion are set to expire. While telehealth proved to be a critical tool during the pandemic, questions remain about reimbursement rates, prescribing rules, and ensuring equitable access for all populations. Lawmakers and regulators are now debating which pandemic-era telehealth policies should be made permanent. The outcome of these discussions will shape the role of virtual care in the U.S. healthcare system for years to come.
