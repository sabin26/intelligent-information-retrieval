The global effort to eradicate polio is facing its final, most difficult challenges. While the disease has been eliminated from most of the world, it remains endemic in a few hard-to-reach areas where conflict and vaccine hesitancy hinder immunization campaigns. Outbreaks of vaccine-derived poliovirus also pose a threat. The article details the strategies being used by the Global Polio Eradication Initiative to overcome these last hurdles and achieve the goal of a polio-free world.
