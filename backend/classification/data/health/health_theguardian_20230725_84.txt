Significant health disparities persist in the United States, with racial and ethnic minority communities experiencing worse health outcomes across a range of conditions. These disparities are driven by a complex mix of systemic factors, including socioeconomic inequality, lack of access to quality healthcare, and implicit bias within the healthcare system. The article highlights the urgent need for policies and interventions that address these root causes. Efforts are focused on improving access, promoting culturally competent care, and tackling the social determinants of health.
