Maternal mortality rates in the United States remain alarmingly high, particularly when compared to other wealthy nations. The problem is especially acute for Black women, who die from pregnancy-related causes at a rate several times higher than white women. The article explores the complex factors contributing to this crisis, including systemic racism in healthcare, lack of access to postpartum care, and the prevalence of chronic health conditions. Experts are calling for urgent, multi-faceted interventions to save mothers' lives.
