A wave of rural hospital closures across the United States is creating 'healthcare deserts' and leaving millions of people without easy access to essential medical services. When a local hospital closes, residents must travel long distances for emergency care, routine check-ups, and specialized treatment. This trend disproportionately affects older and low-income populations and leads to poorer health outcomes for entire communities. The article details the financial pressures forcing these closures and the devastating impact on rural America.
