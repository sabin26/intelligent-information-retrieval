Personalized medicine, which tailors medical treatment to the individual characteristics of each patient, is poised to become the future of healthcare. Advances in genomics and data analytics are allowing doctors to customize treatments based on a person's genetic makeup, lifestyle, and environment. This approach holds the promise of making treatments more effective and reducing side effects. The article explores the progress in fields like oncology and pharmacology and the challenges that remain in bringing personalized medicine into mainstream clinical practice.
